Lesson 4: Senses with which to Enjoy Life

In this lesson, we will explore the importance of our senses, their role in enhancing life experiences, and how they allow us to fully enjoy the world around us.

Reading:

How do we know what is going on in the world around us? There are five main senses that help us enjoy the world. These are the senses of seeing, hear-ing, smelling, touching and taste. With eyes we can see pictures, read words. We can see flowers and birds, the sun and the moon and the stars. We can see our dearest and nearest. With ears we can hear our own voice and the voices of others. We can hear music that makes us feel happy or sad. We are able to distinguish sweet from bitter, sour from fresh with our tongue. With the nose we can smell good food cooking. We can smell flowers. And with the sense of touch one can find out if things are hot or cold. We also have other kinds of senses. Deep inside the body are nerves that tell us of the body needs. For example, we have a sense of hunger and thirst. These two senses let us know when it is time to eat and drink. And we have a muscle sense that helps us compare the weight of two objects. If we lift two boxes of the same size, the muscle sense tells us which box is heavier than the other. The muscle sense also helps us control body movements. The muscle sense helps us walk or ride a bike without looking at our legs. The muscle sense along with the sense of direc-tion, helps us know which way is left and right, up and down, backward and forward. One more sense we have is the sense of balance. In the inner ear are some hollow loops that are filled with liquid. The loops are called semicircular canals. They help us keep the balance when we walk, stand, or sit. When we twirl around, we shake up the liquid in the semicircular canals and then we feel dizzy and unsteady.

Definition: The ability to sense the position and movement of muscles.

8. semicircular canals

Definition: Part of the inner ear that helps maintain balance.
жартылай шеңбер арналар

9. to twirl

Definition: To spin around quickly.
айналу

10. to feel dizzy and unsteady

Definition: To experience a sensation of spinning or losing balance.
бас айналу және тұрақсыздықты сезіну

Grammar :

Understanding Prepositions:

What are Prepositions?

Prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and other words in a sentence. They help us understand time, place, direction, and the way something is done. Prepositions are typically small words, but they have a big impact on meaning.

Example: The cat is under the table.

In this sentence, the word "under" is a preposition that tells us the position of the cat in relation to the table.

Types of Prepositions

  • Prepositions of Place: Show where something is located. (e.g., on, in, under, next to)
  • Prepositions of Time: Indicate when something happens. (e.g., at, on, in, before, after)
  • Prepositions of Direction: Show movement or direction. (e.g., to, into, toward, through)
  • Prepositions of Method: Indicate how something is done. (e.g., by, with, using)

Common Prepositions

  • Place: on, in, under, between, next to
  • Time: at, on, in, during, before, after
  • Direction: to, from, toward, into, out of
  • Method: by, with, using

Prepositions in Sentences

Example 1: The book is on the table. (Preposition of Place)
Example 2: We will meet at 3 PM. (Preposition of Time)
Example 3: She is walking toward the park. (Preposition of Direction)
Example 4: He came by train. (Preposition of Method)

Writing

Objective: Write about the unique features and abilities of the human senses.

Instructions:

  • Describe one or two fascinating senses (e.g., sight, hearing, taste, smell, touch).
  • Explain how these senses contribute to the enjoyment of life and help us interact with the world around us.
  • Include examples of how our senses make everyday experiences richer (e.g., enjoying a meal, listening to music, seeing beautiful landscapes).

Length: 100-150 words

Task 2: Caring for Your Senses

Objective: Write an essay discussing the importance of taking care of your senses to fully enjoy life.

Instructions:

  • Discuss how neglecting our senses can affect our enjoyment of life.
  • Provide examples of simple habits that can improve our sensory experiences, such as maintaining eye health or protecting hearing.
